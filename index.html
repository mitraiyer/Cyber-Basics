CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a fundamental model in cybersecurity that represents Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability.

Confidentiality

Ensuring data privacy and limiting access to authorized users through encryption and access control.

Integrity

Maintaining data accuracy and trustworthiness, often through hashing and validation techniques.

Availability

Ensuring that data and systems are accessible when needed, using redundancy and backup measures.

The Three States of Data

Data can exist in three states: in transit, at rest, and in use. Each state requires different protection strategies.

Data in Transit

Data moving between systems, usually protected by encryption (e.g., HTTPS).

Data at Rest

Data stored on devices, often protected by encryption and physical security.

Data in Use

Data actively being processed; requires access control and monitoring.

Types of Authentication

Authentication verifies user identities and can include methods like passwords, biometrics, and multi-factor authentication (MFA).

Passwords

A string of characters that can include numbers, special characters, and upper/lowercase characters. This is used to verify the identity of a user when they are being authenticated.

Biometrics

The automated recogintion of a user by their unique physical characteristics to verify them during the authenticatio process.

Multi-factor Authentication (MFA)

A security process that requires users to provide multiple methods of verification to get access to a service.

Passwords

Phishing

Phishing scams aim to steal sensitive information by impersonating trustworthy entities. It involves tricking individuals into giving senstive information such as usernames, passwords, and personal details. Deceptive emails or websites that seem legitmate are the most common form of phishing.

Forms of phishing:

Phishing scemes can take various forms, including:

  • Spear Phishing: Targetting specific individuals
  • Voice Phishing: Impersonated a trusted relative/friend to steal information
  • Email Phishing: Sending emails claiming to be legitmate, which can trick the recipient into sending their data.

How to protect yourself

Below are some ways to recognize and protect yourself from phishing schemes:

  • Check email legitimacy through spell check, the email address, and if it is an expected email.
  • Call close friends/relatives to verify they are making a call to you if the call is suspicous.
  • Hover over links and make sure the link address matches the link address on the offical company website.

Potential Threats

Cyber threats can pose a risk for many people. Here are the key potential threats in cybersecurity:

  • Malware: Malicious softwrae that can steal, corrupt, or lock data, compromising systems.
  • Social Engineering: Manipulative tactics that manipulate people into sharing information on how to bypass security protocols.
  • Ransomware: A type of malware that locks files and demands payment for their release.
  • DDoS Attacks: Distrubted Denail of Service that floods servers with traffic, causing disruptions and downtime.

Each of these threats have their own strategies and defeneses to protect them against these risks.
